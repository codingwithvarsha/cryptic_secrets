TYPES OR CATEGORIES

three common types

Hybrids or Chimeras

These creatures combine features from multiple animals or beings. Examples include the griffin (lion and eagle), centaur (human and horse), and chimera (lion, goat, and serpent). Hybrids often possess traits that symbolize strength, wisdom, or other qualities associated with the animals they are composed of.

Shapeshifters or Therianthropes

These creatures have the ability to change their physical form, often between human and animal forms. Examples include werewolves (human and wolf), selkies (seal and human), and skinwalkers (human and various animals in Native American folklore). Shapeshifters are often associated with themes of transformation, duality, and the supernatural.

Elemental or Supernatural Creatures

These creatures embody natural elements or possess supernatural abilities beyond those of ordinary animals. Examples include dragons (fire-breathing reptiles), phoenixes (birds of fire and rebirth), and nymphs (nature spirits). Elemental creatures often symbolize cosmic forces, elemental powers, or aspects of nature beyond human understanding.
